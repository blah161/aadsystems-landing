Semantic Regime Theory™

Semantic Regime Theory™ (SRT™) is a formal framework for modeling, constraining, and executing meaning-bearing systems by defining the rules, transformations, and limits under which semantics operate.

Rather than predicting outcomes statistically, Semantic Regime Theory enforces semantic regimes: deterministic structures that govern how symbols, instructions, policies, and classifications may evolve within a system.

AAD Systems’ AEGON™ family of compilers and classification engines are the first concrete implementations of Semantic Regime Theory™, translating abstract semantic constraints into executable and auditable systems.
